Ancient Times

Telangana, located on the high Deccan Plateau, has a rich and varied history that dates back to ancient times. The region has been ruled by several powerful dynasties, each of which has left its mark on the land and its people. The Satavahana Dynasty, which ruled Telangana from 230 BCE to 220 CE, was one of the most important dynasties in the region's history. The Satavahanas were known for their patronage of the arts, architecture, and literature, and their rule saw the construction of several important monuments and temples.

Telangana has been home to several other powerful dynasties as well. The Kakatiya Dynasty, which ruled from the 12th to the 14th century CE, was known for its military prowess and its impressive architectural achievements, including the famous Warangal Fort. The Chalukyas, who ruled the region for several centuries, were also renowned for their architectural and artistic achievements, and the region is home to several important Chalukyan monuments.

Over the centuries, Telangana has been ruled by several different dynasties, each of which has left its own unique imprint on the region. The Mughals, who ruled the region in the 16th and 17th centuries, were known for their patronage of the arts and architecture, and the region is home to several important Mughal monuments. The Qutubshahis, who ruled the region in the 16th and 17th centuries, were also known for their patronage of the arts, and their rule saw the construction of several important monuments and buildings. Finally, the Asafjahis, who ruled the region from the 18th century onwards, were instrumental in the development of Hyderabad as a major cultural and economic center.

Medieval Period

The medieval history of Telangana is marked by the rule of several powerful dynasties that left a lasting impact on the region. One of the most significant was the Delhi Sultanate, which ruled the region in the 14th and 15th centuries. The Sultanate was known for its military might and its patronage of the arts, and its rule saw the construction of several important monuments and buildings. Telangana was also ruled by the Bahmani Sultanate, which was established in the 14th century and ruled the region until the early 16th century. The Sultanate was known for its tolerance of different religions and its patronage of the arts, and its rule saw the construction of several important monuments and buildings.

During the medieval period, Telangana was also ruled by the Qutb Shahi dynasty, which was founded in the 16th century. The Qutb Shahis were known for their patronage of the arts, and their rule saw the construction of several important monuments and buildings, including the famous Charminar. The dynasty was also known for its military might, and its rule saw several important battles and conflicts.

Finally, the Asafjahis, who ruled the region from the 18th century onwards, were instrumental in the development of Hyderabad as a major cultural and economic center. The Asafjahis were known for their patronage of the arts and literature, and their rule saw the construction of several important monuments and buildings. They were also responsible for the establishment of several important institutions, including modern universities and hospitals. Overall, the medieval period in Telangana was marked by the rule of several powerful dynasties that left a lasting impact on the region's culture, art, and architecture.

Modern Era

On 30 July 2013, the Congress Working Committee unanimously passed a resolution to recommend the formation of a separate Telangana state. After various stages the bill was placed in the Parliament of India in February 2014. In February 2014, Andhra Pradesh Reorganisation Act, 2014 bill was passed by the Parliament of India for the formation of Telangana state comprising ten districts from north-western Andhra Pradesh. The bill received the assent of the President on 1 March 2014.

The state of Telangana was officially formed on 2 June 2014. Kalvakuntla Chandrashekar Rao was elected as the first chief minister of Telangana, following elections in which the Telangana Rashtra Samithi party secured majority. Hyderabad will remain as the joint capital of both Telangana and Andhra Pradesh for a period, not more than 10 years.

Telangana Movement

The Telangana movement was a political movement in India that aimed to create a separate state of Telangana from the pre-existing state of Andhra Pradesh. The movement began in the 1960s and gained momentum in the 2000s, with several protests and agitations taking place. Supporters of the movement cited discrimination in government jobs, education, and public spending as some of the reasons for their demand for a separate state.

The movement was rooted in the historical, economic, social, and cultural identity of Telangana. Proponents of the movement argued that Telangana had a distinct identity and history that was being ignored and oppressed by the larger state of Andhra Pradesh. They also argued that a separate state of Telangana would lead to better governance and development, as resources would be more focused on the needs of the region.

Telangana has also witnessed significant political developments in recent times. The Telangana Rashtra Samithi (TRS) led by K. Chandrashekar Rao has been the dominant political force in the state since its formation. The party has been successful in winning the assembly elections twice, in 2014 and 2018. The opposition parties, including the Congress and the Bharatiya Janata Party (BJP), have been trying to make inroads in the state, but the TRS has maintained its hold on the state politics. Overall, the modern history of Telangana has been marked by significant changes and developments that have transformed the state into a growing hub of industry and commerce in South India.
